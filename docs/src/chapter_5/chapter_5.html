What is a Force?

Gravity is the force which pulls all objects towards the Earth

Force Diagrams

A normal force is a support force exerted by a body in contact with another

Only unbalanced forces cause motion

An applied force is a force that is applied to an object by a person or another object in contact

Satellites are objects that orbit around the Earth or another celestial body.

The satellites which are sent up by humans are known as artificial satellites

The Moon is a natural satellite of Earth

The Moon is held by the Earth's gravity

  • Friction is a force which happens when two objects touch each other.
  • It works against movement, making it harder for things to slide or move.

Friction between your shoes and the ground helps you walk without slipping.

Without friction, your feet would slide everywhere!

Ice is slippery and provides little friction

Friction produces heat when it slows something down!

When you strike a match against the matchbox, friction produces heat, which lights the match.

Heavy objects experience more friction when they slide across the floor

  • Surface texture: Rougher surfaces create more friction than smoother surfaces.
  • Weight: The heavier the object, the more friction.
  • Surface area: Increasing the area of contact between two surfaces increases friction.
  • Water resistance is a type of friction that happens when an object moves through water.
  • It pushes against the object and makes it harder for the object to move quickly in water.
  • The faster an object moves, the stronger the water resistance.
  • The shape of the object affects how much water resistance it feels:
    • A thicker shape feels more resistance.
    • A pointed shape feels less resistance. This shape is called streamlined.

When you swim, you try to make your body more streamlined so you have to do less work.

  • Air resistance is a type of friction that happens when an object moves through air.
  • It pushes against the object, making it harder to move quickly.
  • The faster an object moves, the stronger the air resistance.
  • Objects with large, flat surfaces (like a parachute) feel more air resistance, while streamlined shapes (like an airplane) feel less.

Knowing this helps design objects like cars, planes, and rockets to move more efficiently.

When two or more forces act on an object, we call this multiple forces. These forces can either:

  • Work together to make the object move faster or in the same direction.
  • Work against each other to slow down, stop, or balance the object.
  • Magnets are objects that produce magnetic fields and attract metals like iron, nickel and cobalt.
  • The area around a magnet where its force can be felt is called the magnetic field.
  • Every magnet has two ends called poles: the North Pole (N) and the South Pole (S).
  • Opposite poles attract and same poles repel.

Magnetic materials are substances that are attracted to magnets. Examples include iron, nickel, cobalt.

Non-Magnetic materials are substances that are not attracted to magnets. Examples include wood, plastic, copper, paper
