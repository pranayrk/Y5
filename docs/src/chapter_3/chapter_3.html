What happens when you mix sugar into water?

The sugar dissolves into water

Some solids dissolve in liquids to form a solution

Has the sugar "melted" into the water?

Melting sugar means solid sugar turns into liquid sugar. The liquid is still pure sugar.

Dissolving sugar means solid sugar mixes in with the water to form a mixture of sugar and water.

What happens when sugar dissolves in water?

  • When we dissolve sugar, it does not disappear
  • The sugar breaks up into many tiny parts which are spread throughout the water
  • Sugar (solute) + water (solvent) = sugar (solution)

If we dissolve regular sugar, then the solution is colourless

If we dissolve brown sugar, then the solution is a little brown too

Any solution will always be clear but they may not be colourless.

Why does water in the sea taste salty?

  • Salt is soluble. It dissolved in the water to make a salt solution
  • Salt is the solute, water is the solvent, sea water is the solution

Why does sand not dissolve in water?

  • Not all substances dissolve in all liquids
  • Sand is insoluble in water

If you mix sand and water, the sand does not dissolve in water. The water just becomes cloudy.

How can you seperate a mixture of sand and water?

  • We can wait for the sand to settle down and then pour out the water from the top
  • We can also use a filter or a sieve (or filter) to separate the sand

If you mix salt and water, the salt dissolves in water

Can you separate a mixture of salt and water by sieving?

  • Sieving does not seperate the salt. The salt remains mixed in the water and falls through the holes of the sieve
  • This is because the particles of salt have become too small and are in between the spaces of the water particles
  • Soluble solids cannot be recovered from solutions using filtering

Which of these solids can be seperated from water by filtering?

  • stones
  • sugar
  • sand
  • flour
  • salt
  • peas
Salt cannot be seperated from water by filtering.

So how do we recover salt from water?

  • Salt can be recovered from water by evaporating
  • The water in the solution will evaporate and leave the salt behind
  • When the liquid in a solution evaporates, it leaves behind the solute
