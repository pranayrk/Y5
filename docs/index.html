Topic 1.3: How Seeds are spread

Glossary

explode: to burst open with a lot of force

seed dispersal: when seeds are spread or scattered away from the plant where they are formed

seedlings: the new plants that grow from a germinated seed

spongy: like a sponge with soft holes that can be filled with air or water

germinated: a seed that has just begun to grow

Topic 1.3: How Seeds are spread

Questions, Page 17

Q.1 Which seeds would grow better: those in a tomato left on the plant, or those in a tomato eaten by a bird? Say why.

The seeds eaten by the bird would grow better.
The bird would spread the seeds in its droppings away from the parent plant.
The seedlings would not be overcrowded like the seedlings that germinate right next to the parent plant.
They would be able to get more light and water for growth.

A peach seed

Q.2 Do you think peach seeds can be dispersed by animals? Say why or why not.

No. Animals do not eat peach seeds which are big and hard. The seeds do not have spikes or hooks either. The seeds are also slightly poisonous.

Q.3 Make a drawing of a seed you think would be dispered in an animal's fur.

The seed will have spines or hooks or spikes that can get caught in an animal's fur.

Q.4 How can people disperse seeds?

People disperse seeds by planting them in their gardens or farms.

Questions, Page 19

Water lily seeds

Q.1 A water lily seed has a spongy covering. How does this help a water lily disperse its seeds?

The spongy seed coat has air spaces that trap air and help the seed to float. (water disperal)

Bean Pods

Q.2 Why are bean pods usually picked before the weather gets hot and dry?

If the weather becomes hot and dry, the pods explode and we can't pick them to eat.

Q.3 Apple seeds are dispersed by the drop-and-roll method. How else can the apple seeds be spread?

Animals can eat the fruit and disperse the seeds in their droppings. (animal dispersal)

Maple seeds

Q.4 How are the seeds in this picture adapted for wind dispersal?

They have papery wings which help them blow away easily. (wind dispersal)
