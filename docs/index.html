Question: Give some examples of non-flowering plants.

Plants such as ferns and mosses don't have flowers. They produce spores instead of seeds.

Spores are tiny specks of living material

Mosses have spores that are found inside a capsule

These spores are dispersed by the wind

Ferns have feathery leaves and their stems are underground

Their spores are found on the underside of their leaves

Q.1 Which seeds would grow better: those in a tomato left on the plant, or those in a tomato eaten by a bird? Say why.

The seeds eaten by the bird would grow better.
The bird would spread the seeds in its droppings away from the parent plant.
The seedlings would not be overcrowded like the seedlings that germinate right next to the parent plant.
They would be able to get more light and water for growth.

# Chapter 2: Sound

The air around us is made of particles. Sound is just vibrating air particles.

### What is sound? * Sound is a form of energy that can be heard and travels through waves * When matter **vibrates** or moves back and forth very quickly, a sound is made * Sound waves can travel through solids, liquids or gases * **Example:** When a bell rings, parts of the bell will vibrate, creating a sound
### What are sound waves? * A wave carries energy from one place to another without transferring matter * An object that is vibrating in the air produces a sound wave * The vibrating object causes air molecules to move back and forth
### How do we make sound? * When you speak, the **vocal cords** in your throat **vibrate** * These vibrations cause sound waves to travel to their ears * The other person's ears interpret these sound waves
